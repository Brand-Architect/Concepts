Critical

No logo — just a text header

The site header displays plain text with no mark, no icon and no visual identity. For an award-winning roastery this is a fundamental credibility problem from the very first second a visitor arrives.

Critical

Three different brand names in use

The browser tab says "Rin's Coffee". The header reads "Rinaldo's Speciality Coffee & Tea". Social uses "RinsCoffee". Three names in circulation means customers — and search engines — don't know what to call you.

Critical

The hero undersells the brand entirely

"Consistently Excellent Coffee" sets the lowest possible bar. You have an award-winning house blend, a real roastery and a genuine story rooted in the Lake District. None of that appears above the fold.

Needs attention

Brand colour is completely absent

The distinctive crimson that defines your new identity appears nowhere on the website. Every button and accent defaults to Shopify grey. The brand has no visual personality online whatsoever.

Needs attention

Navigation is inconsistent and cluttered

The nav mixes ALL CAPS, Title Case and sentence case with no rule applied. A HOME link wastes a slot the logo should handle. The structure doesn't reflect how customers actually browse and buy.

Needs attention

Category pages have no editorial voice

SPECIALITY COFFEES / FINE TEAS / BREWING KIT float over images with no story, no context and no reason for a customer to choose one section over another.

Minor

Announcement bar reads as a logistics notice

"FREE DELIVERY ON ORDERS OVER £35 — DISPATCHED 5 DAYS A WEEK" is purely functional copy. A brand with genuine warmth should use that prime real estate to build a relationship, not recite schedules.

Minor

Newsletter section lacks a real offer

"Sign up and save!" without saying save what or how much is boilerplate nobody believes. A genuine offer tied to your story would convert far better.

Minor

"Powered by Shopify" is still showing

One line to remove in theme settings — but leaving it in signals an unfinished site for a brand positioning itself at the premium end of specialty coffee.

02

The new brand direction

Where your brand is going — and why it will work harder for you across every touchpoint, from the website to the packaging on your coffee bags.

Premium, warm
and genuinely yours.

The new identity is built around a distinctive monogram mark that can stand alone on packaging, cups, embossing and social — and a wordmark that communicates the same quality as the coffee inside the bag. The palette draws from the warmth of the roasting process itself. The typography balances heritage craftsmanship with modern legibility. Every decision has been made to feel like Rin's — and nobody else.
