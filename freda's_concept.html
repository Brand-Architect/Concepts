The Opportunity

A brand with Cornish soul — and a digital presence that feels generic.

Freda's isn't just peanut butter. It's a small-batch, handmade product from the coast of Cornwall. Currently, the brand is presented through standard e-commerce patterns that hide its unique provenance. The identity should be as rich as the product itself.

01

The Existing Mark

The current script logo lacks the "artisan" authority required for a premium product. It feels like a standard template font rather than a bespoke identity that signals quality and heritage.

02

Visual Clutter

The homepage is overwhelmed by discount banners and competing calls to action. A premium brand should lead with its story and quality, not with a bargain-first mentality.

03

Buried Heritage

The Cornish origin is a massive USP, but it's secondary to sales-driven messaging. The brand needs to "own" its location and the handcrafted nature of its process.

04

Product Presentation

Inconsistent photography and generic layouts don't reflect the premium price point. The presentation should feel as considered as the ingredients inside the jar.

The Cornish Craft

Freda's was born from a desire for a better nut butter. No shortcuts, no fillers, just the best ingredients roasted to perfection in the Cornish sea air. That integrity deserves an identity to match.

The new identity moves away from the "cottage industry" look and towards a "modern classic" aesthetic. It's a brand that feels at home in a high-end deli in London or a surf shop in St Ives.
