The Brand

Six Great Taste Awards.
One identity to match.

Altitude Coffee London is a Great Taste Award-winning speciality roaster operating from Hornchurch. Every batch is roasted by hand in small quantities, shipped within 48 hours, and delivered in fully plastic-free packaging.

The brand stands for something specific: ethical sourcing at twice the Fair Trade price, direct relationships with growers, and a rigorous approach to flavour. What it has lacked, until now, is a visual identity built to carry all of that.

The Concept

A mark with direction built into its structure

The crossbar of the A has been redrawn as a rising chevron — not as decoration, but as architecture. Every brand element that follows should carry the same upward intent.

01 / The Logomark

Built Around Your Name

The mark is drawn from the name itself — not a generic symbol placed beside it. It belongs entirely to Altitude Coffee London and could belong to no one else.

02 / The Wordmark

Presence at Every Scale

The wordmark is designed to command attention whether it appears on a coffee bag, a cup, or a storefront. Bold, considered, and unmistakably yours.

03 / The System

Rooted in London

The identity places you firmly in London — not as a footnote, but as a statement of provenance. In the speciality coffee world, origin carries authority. This mark wears yours with pride.

Brand Values

What the identity must carry

Award-Winning Quality

Six Great Taste Awards from the Guild of Fine Food, earned consecutively from 2021 to 2024. The identity should communicate that standard without needing to explain it.

Ethical at the Source

Farmers are paid twice the Fair Trade price. Long-term relationships replace transactional purchasing. The mark should feel like something worth that commitment.

Zero-Plastic Packaging

Every bag, envelope, and label is plastic-free. An identity built on this principle should itself feel considered — nothing superfluous, nothing wasteful.

Freshness Without Compromise

Roasted to order and shipped within 48 hours. The identity should carry that energy — something alive, immediate, and made with visible care.
