The Opportunity

Already in The Conran Shop and the British Library.
The identity should match.

Rye Chocolates has done something genuinely difficult: built a handmade, single-origin chocolate brand stocked in two of Britain's most discerning retail environments. That is the outcome of years of craft, consistency, and quality.

The current visual identity, however, does not communicate that level of achievement. For a buyer at The Conran Shop or a customer in the British Library gift hall, the packaging and brand presentation is the first — and often only — signal of what the product is worth.

This concept explores what Rye Chocolates could look like if the identity were built to the same standard as the chocolate itself.

The Gap

Current Identity

A local chocolate shop with ambition

  • Visual language that reads as artisan-local rather than artisan-premium
  • No clear typographic system across touchpoints
  • Brand presence that undersells the product and its provenance
  • No consistent mark or monogram for packaging, stamps, or embossing
  • Identity that could belong to any high-street confectionery brand

With a Considered Identity

A British artisan brand fit for international retail

  • A monogram mark — RC — that holds on foil-stamped packaging, wax seals, and gift tissue
  • Typographic hierarchy drawing on the tradition of British luxury goods
  • Colour and material language that signals single-origin provenance with confidence
  • An identity built to scale across Rye Chocolates, Only Coco, and future expansion
  • Shelf presence in The Conran Shop that earns its position beside peers

Brand Applications

01
Packaging

Bar Wrappers & Outer

The RC monogram foil-stamped on dark kraft or glassine wrappers. Each origin colour-coded within the gold palette. Clean, unhurried, shelf-confident.

02
Gifting

Gift Boxes & Tissue

The monogram repeated as a refined pattern across tissue paper. Ribbon in Ivory Press. Box lid embossed — no print, no colour, just depth.

03
Wholesale

Trade & Stockist Collateral

Order sheets, shelf-talkers, and point-of-sale materials that speak the same language as The Conran Shop's own aesthetic — restrained, authoritative.

04
Digital

Web & Social Presence

A visual language that carries into digital without dilution. Dark backgrounds, single-origin photography, gold accents used sparingly and deliberately.

05
In-store

Shop Environment

Signage, chalkboards, and tasting cards in a unified hand. The RC monogram as a wax seal on handwritten notes. Every touchpoint coherent.

06
Expansion

Only Coco Sub-brand

A sister identity that shares the typographic system and provenance language, differentiated by palette — allowing both brands to grow without conflict.

Built with the same care
as the chocolate itself.

Rye Chocolates is already producing at a level that commands serious retail attention. The identity should open doors — not ask them to forgive the packaging. This concept is an invitation to explore what that looks like.
