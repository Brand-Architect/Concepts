The Opportunity

A brand with extraordinary provenance — and an identity that doesn't yet reflect it.

Caribe Coffee Co. roasts directly from Honduran family farms. They turned down Peter Jones on Dragon's Den to stay true to their vision. They have 30,000 loyal customers and a genuine origin story. The brand they show the world should be equal to that story.

01

The Existing Mark

Their current logo is a palm tree, which has nothing to do with coffee. Coffee does not come from palm trees. It is a totally illogical logo design that costs the mark its authority before it even begins.

02

The Homepage

The Dragon's Den appearance leads every page. A premium roastery shouldn't open with a television credential — it should open with coffee.

03

The Product Imagery

AI-generated packaging imagery is inconsistent and at odds with the handcrafted story. The quality of the coffee isn't in question. The quality of how it's presented is.

04

The Missed Opportunity

Direct from Honduran family farms. Microlot single estates. SHG grading. These are premium signals buried beneath discount banners.

The Story Beneath the Brand

Caribe began at a market table in Morpeth. It grew through the integrity of what was in the bag. That integrity deserves an identity to match.

The Dragon's Den moment matters. Not as a headline — as a footnote. A brand with this provenance leads with the farm, the altitude, the harvest.

The identity proposed here is built on a single premise: the C as vessel, the name as pour. One continuous read. No embellishment needed.

Typography

Type that signals standing.

Premium brands don't announce themselves. They signal. Typography is the first signal — before a word is read. The proposed type system pairs a classical display face with a refined sans.
